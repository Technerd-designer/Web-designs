HIV FREE YOUTH

Priority areas included:

  • Prevention
  • Advocacy
  • IEC materials development
  • Institutional capacity building
  • Acceptability of condoms promotion and distribution among 13-19 year old adolescents for HIV/AIDS and Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights(SRHR) prevention in Ilemela,Kasulu and Kibondo.


The main objective was to equip youth aged between 13-24 years with comprehensive knowledge on HIV/AIDS and ensure skill-based HIV/AIDS and SRHR media and initiatives are integrated into youth school extra curricular activities.


Strategies of the project include:

  • Mobilization and packaging of youth-friendly HIV/SRHR popular publications materials
  • HIV/SHRH knowledge popularized to youth by youth through media(print and electronic) and performed arts initiatives
  • Monitoring,evaluation and sharing of best practices


Outcomes of the project were:

  • Increased number of schools with active integration of HIV/SHRH education into students' acheduled extra curricular activities
  • Improved access to HIV/SHRH information to youth
  • Increased comprehensive knowledge of HIV/SHRH among youth
  • Reduced number of HIV infections among youth aged 13-24 in the target area


The donor was Dr Derick of Shelby Foundation,Australia

DISABILITY-INCLUSIVE COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT FOR PREVENTION AND TREATMENT OF HIV AND REDUCING STIGMA IN KASULU,KIGOMA

This event kicked off at Fatina LAAY Conference Hall,Kasulu with His Excellency Col.Isaack Mwakisu,the Kasulu District Commissioner as the official guest of honor.
The President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief in the United States funded the project.


We have been awarded the contract to implement it in four Kasulu town council facilities:

  • Kiganamo Health Center(Murubona Ward)
  • Kasulu Hospital(Kimobwa Ward)
  • Heru juu Health Centre(Heru juu Ward)
  • Marumba Health Centre(Muhunga Ward)


The objectives and expected outcomes are:

  • Improving HIV prevention and treatment
  • To increase community awareness on HIV prevention and treatment
  • To reduce stigma and discrimination in accessing HIV Health Care Services
